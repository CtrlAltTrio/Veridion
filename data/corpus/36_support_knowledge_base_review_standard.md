# Knowledge Base Review Standard

**Owner:** Support Enablement
**Last reviewed:** 2026-03-11
**Applies to:** Northwind Systems employees and relevant customer workspaces

## Purpose

This standard keeps customer-facing troubleshooting content current and accountable. It is the controlled internal source for this topic. Northwind Systems uses it to make decisions consistently across Portland, Austin, Dublin, and remote teams. Readers should check the owner and review date before acting, particularly when a customer contract, regional rule, or active incident may affect the normal workflow.

## Authoritative facts

- Every knowledge article is reviewed at least every 90 days.
- Articles with more than 5,000 monthly views receive a monthly accuracy check.
- A broken procedure is unpublished within four business hours of confirmation.
- Article owners record the test workspace and product version used for validation.

These values are deliberate operating limits, not examples. Teams must not round a deadline, substitute a different threshold, or promise an exception unless the listed owner approves it through the documented channel. Where two records appear to disagree, the signed customer agreement controls commercial terms and the newer approved internal policy controls general operations.

## Operating procedure

The case owner records timestamps, customer impact, troubleshooting evidence, and each handoff in the support system. Urgent work follows the published severity route; routine requests stay in the normal queue. Support must verify the requester before disclosing account information or changing configuration. If the runbook does not cover the observed condition, the duty manager selects the next team and records the reason.

Before closing the work, the responsible person verifies the relevant identifier, date, amount, plan, region, or severity and attaches enough evidence for another employee to reproduce the decision. Automated reminders support the process but do not replace owner accountability. A missed target is escalated on the same business day rather than hidden by changing a timestamp.

## Exceptions and escalation

Exceptions are narrow, time-bound, and written. The request describes the business reason, risk, affected people or workspaces, proposed end date, and the control that reduces exposure. Silence is not approval. Emergency action may proceed to protect people, customer data, or service availability, but the acting lead records the decision within one business day and informs the policy owner.

## Evidence and maintenance

The duty manager samples completed cases each month for correct severity, timestamps, verification, and customer communication. Required evidence must remain attached to the case for two years. Runbook defects are reported to Support Enablement and corrected within five business days. A post-incident action does not change the published target until the owner approves a revision.

# Company Profile and Operating Calendar

**Owner:** Office of the COO
**Last reviewed:** 2026-05-15
**Applies to:** Northwind Systems employees and relevant customer workspaces

## Purpose

This page gives employees a stable reference for Northwind Systems company facts and the calendar used for planning. It is the controlled internal source for this topic. Northwind Systems uses it to make decisions consistently across Portland, Austin, Dublin, and remote teams. Readers should check the owner and review date before acting, particularly when a customer contract, regional rule, or active incident may affect the normal workflow.

## Authoritative facts

- Northwind Systems was founded on 18 April 2016.
- The headquarters is in Portland, Oregon, with operating offices in Austin, Texas, and Dublin, Ireland.
- The company has 420 employees as of 30 June 2026.
- The fiscal year runs from 1 January through 31 December.

These values are deliberate operating limits, not examples. Teams must not round a deadline, substitute a different threshold, or promise an exception unless the listed owner approves it through the documented channel. Where two records appear to disagree, the signed customer agreement controls commercial terms and the newer approved internal policy controls general operations.

## Operating procedure

Employees apply this guidance during routine planning and raise uncertainty with their manager before making a commitment. Managers record approvals in the system named in the policy rather than relying on a private message. People Operations or the listed owner resolves interpretation questions. When local law or an executed agreement provides a stronger benefit, the stronger requirement controls and the owner documents the exception.

Before closing the work, the responsible person verifies the relevant identifier, date, amount, plan, region, or severity and attaches enough evidence for another employee to reproduce the decision. Automated reminders support the process but do not replace owner accountability. A missed target is escalated on the same business day rather than hidden by changing a timestamp.

## Exceptions and escalation

Exceptions are narrow, time-bound, and written. The request describes the business reason, risk, affected people or workspaces, proposed end date, and the control that reduces exposure. Silence is not approval. Emergency action may proceed to protect people, customer data, or service availability, but the acting lead records the decision within one business day and informs the policy owner.

## Evidence and maintenance

The policy owner reviews this page annually and after a material organizational change. Evidence includes approvals, register entries, receipts, or service-desk records as applicable. Employees should link to this page rather than copying its text into local handbooks, because copied versions are not controlled. Questions and proposed corrections go to the listed owner, who records a revision date when facts change.
